Building a corporate identity

Home / Service / Building a corporate identity

The corporate identity is the main tool that represents your business in the marketplace among various competitors. A strong corporate identity is a must for every organization as it aids in customer and company interaction. Clients usually get a lot of help in understanding business with the help of corporate identity. It includes many supporting devices, such as company letterhead, business cards, website etc.

Benefits of corporate identity

The logo is the most visible aspect of the corporate identity. Thus, a good logo differentiates your organization among the competition and provides the highest level of visibility.

Your corporate identity helps you increase ownership and control as well. As customers now have awareness about your business and its services, they can understand your ownership standards and control your organization.

Your company's image and reputation is also an important component of your company's growth. The image and reputation of your company depends on the quality of the services you provide, and the quality of services depends on the method of your strategic activities and your marketing plans.

In building the corporate identity, we shed light on the basic axes of the institution, starting from its vision, mission, smart goals, human values, brand name and ideal customers, so that we can draw the complete mental picture of its personality and competitive advantages in order to obtain
A unique identity that suits the target group

Trademark registration

General trademarks

They are the least types in terms of obtaining legal protection, as any person can use the same word in marketing his product without problems, so it is not usually registered because it may be a sentence or a specific slogan.

Descriptive trademarks

used to describe a product, commodity, or institution, and it is also one of the weakest types of trademarks in terms of the protection they enjoy.

General trademarks

This type of trademark is the most protective, so these trademarks have been invented from scratch and there is no similarity at all, and it may be a sentence or a slogan that was specially invented to promote a certain type of goods or products, and it is linked to it alone directly.
